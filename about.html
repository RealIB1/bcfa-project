About Us

Blockchain Foundation Africa is a non-profit organization created to empower young entrepreneurs, who are the intellectual, social, and cultural life's blood of a country - in this case, Ghana. Blockchain Foundation Africa will be raising Bitcoin funds for projects that will build a bright future for our country by bringing together all young, ambitious, and self-determining entrepreneurs and enabling them to achieve their dreams, without the need for government assistance.
The Foundation will enable them to establish their various businesses online to serve the international market, and their mode of payment will be through Cryptos, which will be the means by which their dream businesses will become a reality and a viable and resourceful presence in the world. This will, in turn, enable Ghana to fulfill her dreams and potential in the global community.

Our Team

Philip Agyei Asare

Philip Agyei Asare is an Entrepreneur and a Philantropist who has been active in the Decentralised Finance, Cryptocurrency and Blockchain Industry since 2013. He is the founder of Ghana’s first Bitcoin/Blockchain-based remittance company BTC Ghana (now Inqoins) which has been in operations since 2013. Philip is the co-founder of Nano Africa- A grassroots organization based in Ghana who are building a Nano Community in Africa. He founded Dream Bitcoin Foundation (now Blockchain Foundation Africa) to educate and inform Africans on the growing Decentralized Finance and Cryptocurrency industry. He is the initiator of Coinfest Ghana which has been running since 2015. He pioneered the first Bitcoin conference in Africa in 2014 Philip is a prolific speaker and has given talks on Cryptocurrency adoption in Africa at several conferences within Africa in countries such as Cote D’Ivoire, Sierra Leone, Gambia, Nigeria etc and has been guest at Global Crypto Conferences in Dubai and Singapore. A true technologist at heart, he founded one of Ghana’s first SMS broadcast companies and has adviced and consulted for many technology companies on their expansion to Ghana.

Narigamba Mwinsuuboe

Narigamba Mwinsuubo is a Cryptopreneur, Investor, Advisor, and the Founder of Bitland, an organization that uses blockchain technology for land titles and helps Ghanaians attain proof of ownership property rights and secure a more financially stable future. He's been active with other blockchain projects such Bitnation in 2014 and a board member for Gamblica and CryptoTrust for the past years. He is currently working on digital security and tracking of land titles launching the pilot service in Ghana due to a local need for autonomy through improved land rights and was the first to ever put a land title document on the blockchain in Ghana.

Kwaku Abedi

Kwaku Abedi is a content developer for Blockhain and Cryptocurrency-focused businesses. He is the founder of the Africa Blockchain Report – a blog focused on practical ways Blockchain Technology and Cryptocurrency can be integrated into the African Economic ecosystem for the greater good. He is a freelance content developer and writer and has worked for online magazines in the Travel, Business and Self-Development space. Kwaku has a passion of aligning African economies to current digital and Decentralized Finance (DeFi) systems such as cryptocurrency, altcoins and Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) through media to create awareness on the African continent. He has developed content Blockchain Foundation Africa, NewsBTC, BTC Ghana (Inqoins), Nano etc. His work has been published in the Business & Financial Times Newspaper
